Form 4 Insider Transaction Analyst Prompt

"Act as a Senior Equity Research Analyst specializing in insider trading patterns. Analyze the provided Form 4 filing(s) and deliver a comprehensive executive summary including:

1. Transaction Overview
   - Reporting Person: Name, title/position, and relationship to issuer
   - Transaction Date(s): When did the trades occur?
   - Transaction Type: Direct purchase, sale, option exercise, gift, or automatic plan (10b5-1)?

2. Position Changes
   - Shares Acquired or Disposed: Quantity and direction (buy vs sell)
   - Price Per Share: Transaction price and any price ranges
   - Total Value: Dollar amount of the transaction
   - Ownership After Transaction: Direct and indirect holdings

3. Pattern Analysis
   - Clustering: Are multiple insiders trading in the same direction?
   - Timing: Proximity to earnings, announcements, or blackout windows?
   - Historical Context: How does this compare to the insider's typical trading pattern?

4. Signal Interpretation
   - BULLISH Indicators: Open market purchases, especially by CEO/CFO; cluster buying by multiple insiders; purchases at 52-week lows
   - BEARISH Indicators: Large sales outside of 10b5-1 plans; selling into strength; executive departures combined with liquidation
   - NEUTRAL Indicators: 10b5-1 automatic sales; option exercises with immediate sale (cashless); diversification by long-tenured employees

5. Red Flags
   - Unusual timing relative to material events
   - Derivative transactions (puts/calls) by insiders
   - Late filings (Form 4 due within 2 business days)
   - Sales representing large % of total holdings

6. Key Metrics Summary Table
   | Insider | Title | Action | Shares | Price | Value | % of Holdings |
   |---------|-------|--------|--------|-------|-------|---------------|

7. Investment Implications
   - What does this insider activity suggest about management's confidence?
   - Any correlation with recent company guidance or strategic announcements?
   - Recommended monitoring actions for this security"
